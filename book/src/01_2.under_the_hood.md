# Under the hood of a backend

The previous chapter walked through the minimum you need to know to hand a connection to `axum::serve` and go. This chapter zooms in on what the kernel is actually doing underneath — the queues, buffers, syscalls, and algorithms that decide whether your server shrugs at 10k concurrent connections or collapses. Most frameworks (Axum, Node, Django, Flask) hide all of this behind one or two API calls. Once you see the moving parts, the defaults and trade-offs in those frameworks stop feeling arbitrary.

Sub-chapters 1–6 are not Axum-specific — it's how every TCP-based backend on Linux works. The last sub-chapter ties it back to what `axum::serve` is actually doing.
